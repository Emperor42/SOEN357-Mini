    Existing Applications & Services

    Flora

    Grow trees to stay calm and focused

    Flora is a multi-platform app and browser extension which helps people to focus on their lives offline. The user selects a duration and is not able to use their distracting apps and games for this period without killing a digital tree. The app has different tasks which allow users to "discover" new trees. Flora includes social features allowing for a user to select friends to collectively plant trees, should anyone use their device in the given period the tree will die. Flora includes both to-do-list functions and a time management helper to assist users in developing positive habits. Flora has through partnerships to provide an opt-in service where their users are able to use the app the plant real trees worldwide.

    Google Fit

    Activity goals to improve health

    Google Fit is a free to use Android app which provides coaching for a more healthy lifestyle.  Google has collaborated with the WHO to develop Heart Points, which are an easy to understand metric for various type of activity.  Google Fit provides a series of actionable tips based on the information you have shared with the app over time directly to your phone or smartwatch. Google Fit works automatically with a variety of sensors available on Android devices to improve the overall view of  health to which the user has access. Google Fit also includes a series of integrations with popular fitness apps available on the Android platform.

    Flipd

    Productivity and happiness all-in-one

    Flipd is a phone application focused on improving the productivity and well-being of its users. Flipd provides a variety of timers which can then assist a keeping users on track with their goals. Flipd allows for connections to real people for studying and other tasks, taking breaks and free background music. Flipd provides stats regarding progress over various time periods and the ability to track milestones and tag activity based on categories. Flipd creates a community of friendly competition through a leader-board which encourages motivational support for others in the community. One of the key features of Flipd is their Focus Lock, which prevents users from accessing apps which are not Flipd without explicitly taking a break from their task.

    Space

    Find your phone / life balance

    Space is a mobile application which assists users in beating mobile phone addiction. Space provides a user with opportunities to know the impact the phone has on their lives. Space allows a user to track and set a series of daily goals to be able to better manage their phone habits. Space allows comparisons with users contacts or the general public which can allow for a level of friendly competition. Space provides a reward system, achievements, and a toolkit to assist users in reducing their phone use. Space provides access to research completed by their team, quotes and tips to assist users in maintaining a healthy phone / life balance.

    Research

    Introduction

    With the advent of smartphones and the increased popularity of computers, modern lifestyles have become increasingly sedentary. More and more people are working office or customer services jobs that don’t require much physical activity. Given the time investment of working the customary forty hours a week, there isn’t much free time for the average person.

    The Problem

    As people are lacking time, they tend to choose faster alternatives such as eating fast food rather than cooking a meal at home. Rather than planning a workout routine and going through with it, people are devoting more time to other activities. Not only are these issues leading to an unhealthy population, modern screens produce blue light which reduces sleep quality and can slowly damage the eyesight of users.

    Our Solution

    As people are already looking at their phones, having a one-stop app is immensely convenient to track and reinforce good habits. Fitnesse will include the ability to search through a variety of third party lifestyle apps and set daily goals. Not only that, but the app will soft limit users’ screen time by monitoring and notifying the user when they have surpassed their chosen daily value.

    Surveys

    We used surveys to understand what potential users view their current fitness levels, what they want to improve and how interested they are in a potential fitness app. The answers allowed us to understand which demographics would be our target audiences and how the different demographics had different needs.
    We found that a decent amount of surveyors (33.3%) already use apps to track and manage their fitness and that a significant portion of surveyors (41.7%) were interested in having a fitness app. Having 75% of surveyors being interested in fitness apps shows a general interest for our product, especially considering the lack of a fitness super app.

    Additionally, surveyors were generally satisfied with how often they worked out  (50%)  but not with how often they ate healthy (20%).

    Personas

    Based on the results of our surveys, we created three different user personas that we will be targeting with our app. We believe that the majority of our users will fall into these three archetypes. All of our personas are fictional people.
    The first persona of the three is Marie Waters. She is a middle aged woman who is the mother of two. As a user, she is someone looking to better themselves across the board but has trouble finding the time to plan a regiment for herself and sticking to it.
    The second persona is DeMarcus Nelson. He is a young adult who works out consistently but wants to improve other aspects of his health profile. He is used to sticking with training routines, but needs help regulating his screen time and managing his sleep schedule. 
    The last persona is Dustin Davis. Similarly to DeMarcus Nelson, he is a young adult, though he is not used to adhering to his training regiment, though he does work out semi regularly. This type of user will benefit from all the assets included in our super app.

    User Goals

    The surveys and the personas have let us distill what the goals of users are going to be. Generally users will be managing their health profile, which includes sleep schedule, diet, training regiment and managing screen time. However, the hidden goal is helping the user discover new regiments while adhering to their chosen regiment.

    User Flow Diagram

    The beginning of our design process was the user flow diagram. Once we had conceptualized our goals for the app, we began to design how the user would interact with it from their point of view. This diagram shows the experience loop that the users will be in when on the app.

    Detailed Sketches

    Once the user flow diagram had been finalised, we began to sketch out how the app will look and how it will be laid out. The sketches were not a finalised render of the project as the app is subject to changes. These detailed sketches were created in place of wire-frames as creating different wire-frames for the popular mobile resolutions as the detailed sketches contain all the pertinent information to create the prototype.

    Colour Palette

    The colour palette is inspired from the current trend of pastel colours. However, some changes were made from the usual white and cool colours as we wanted to avoid emitting blue light and in turn ease the strain on eyes. We chose a soft grey for our background colour with warm colours for the accents. We did include a pastel purple which is a cool colour, but we believe that with our other decisions it will not impact the viewers eyes. The grey was chosen as white for a background is quite damaging to the eyes.

    Typography

    We chose Arial Narrow and Lato as our font due to its simplicity and elegance. Both appear very similar to each-other, Lato is standard in many Bootstrap applications and Arial Narrow is also a standard font that has been integrated in the Microsoft font suite for decades now. We believe that having a simple and familiar font will help instil a sense of confidence in the users for our app.

    Conclusion

    The design process for this task has been interesting despite being challenging, as super apps are still a novel concept.  Devising the layout of an application with multiple different target audiences proved to be easier said than done. We researched other health apps and interface trends to develop our own interface despite our research being on apps with a narrower focus. We sought to simplify the experience for our users with a minimalistic design.
